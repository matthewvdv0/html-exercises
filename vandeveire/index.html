    If youâ€™re recovering from a knee injury or surgery, a well-structured knee conditioning program can help you regain strength, flexibility, and range of motion. Here are some key points to consider:

    Summary: Strengthen and stretch your knee muscles to promote recovery and prevent future injuries.

    1. Strength Training:

    Strengthening the muscles that support your knee is crucial. Strong muscles reduce stress on the knee joint and help absorb shock. Focus on the following muscle groups:

    • Hamstrings

    • Quadtracepts

    • Abductors & Adductors

    • Gluteus medius & Gluteus maximus

    2. Flexibility

    Stretching the muscles you strengthen is essential for restoring range of motion and preventing injury. After strength exercises, gently stretch to reduce muscle soreness and maintain flexibility.

    3. Warmup

    Before starting exercises, warm up with 5 to 10 minutes of low-impact activity like walking or stationary cycling.

    4. Exercises:

      Helcord Stretch:

    1. Stand facing a wall with your unaffected leg forward and a slight bend at the knee.

    2. Your affected leg should be straight and behind you, heel flat and toes slightly pointed inward.

    3. Press your hips forward toward the wall, keeping both heels flat on the floor.

    4. Hold this stretch for 30 seconds, then relax for 30 seconds. Repeat.

      Standing Quadtracepts Stretch:

    1. Hold on to the back of a chair or a wall for balance.

    2. Stretch the front of your thigh by bending your knee and bringing your heel toward your buttocks.

    3. Hold for 30 seconds, then relax. Repeat.
